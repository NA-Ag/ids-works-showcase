 1. The "Hook" (ROI Calculator)
   * Strength: The "Simulador de Overhead Operativo" is your strongest sales asset. It shifts the conversation from "How much does this cost?" to "How much am I currently losing?"
   * Critique: The fixed width (min-w-[700px]) in the table might make it difficult to demo on a tablet or phone during an in-person meeting. Also, the "Ahorro Anual" is impressive, but adding a "Payback
     Period" (e.g., "Investment recovered in 8 months") would be a powerful closing metric.


  2. Demo Strategy
   * Strength: Having three distinct tiers (Lite, Standard, Integrated) is excellent for "Price Anchoring." Most customers will naturally gravitate toward the middle "Standard" tier.
   * Critique: Once a user enters a demo (e.g., StandardDemo), they are "trapped" in the simulated school. From a sales perspective, you need a "Sales Overlay" or a persistent "Back to IDS Works / Hire Us"
     button that floats over the demo. Otherwise, the lead might forget they are on a vendor site.


  3. Content & Localization
   * Strength: The "Colegio IDS" data is very detailed (Accreditations, Headmaster message, etc.), which builds high trust.
   * Critique: I noticed a slight "identity crisis" in the code. Some marketing constants are hardcoded in Spanish (constants.tsx), while the demo data (schoolData.ts) is fully bilingual. If you are selling
     to "Colegios Biculturales," the main landing page must be as bilingual as the product you're selling.


  4. Technical Impression (The "Look & Feel")
   * Strength: The "Vault" theme (Dark Blue/Yellow) feels secure and "Ivy League," which fits the educational sector perfectly.
   * Critique: The use of Unsplash images is okay for a prototype, but for a high-ticket sale ($300k+ MXN), the demos should feel less like "templates" and more like "blueprints." The "Integrated" demo
     currently feels very similar to the "Standard" one; it needs more visual cues for the "Automation" and "Payment" modules you're promising.


  5. Conversion Path
   * Strength: Clear CTAs in the pricing table.
   * Critique: The "Contact" form is standard. For a high-ticket B2B service, adding a "Download Technical PDF" (which I see you have a generate_pdf.cjs for) as a lead magnet would be a great way to capture
     emails from directors who aren't ready to call yet.


  Summary: You have a "Ferrari" under the hood with the ROI logic and the multi-tier routing. To make it a "Closing Machine," we should focus on navigation continuity (not getting lost in the demo) and
  deepening the Integrated tier's features.

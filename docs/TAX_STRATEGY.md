# Tax Strategy & Legal Framework (Mexico)

As a solo developer selling custom software infrastructure and digital assets (high-ticket websites) to schools in Mexico, choosing the correct tax regime (Régimen Fiscal) is critical to maximizing your profit margin, especially since you are collecting 50% upfront.

Since you are not a registered agency (Persona Moral/S.A. de C.V.) and are operating as an independent professional (Persona Física), you have two primary options under Mexican Tax Law (SAT). 

## The Winner: RESICO (Régimen Simplificado de Confianza)
For a solo developer selling high-ticket digital assets with very low overhead costs (you don't buy physical raw materials to build code), **RESICO is the absolute best legal option.**

### Why RESICO?
*   **Insanely Low Income Tax (ISR):** Instead of paying up to 35% on your profits, RESICO charges a flat rate of **1% to 2.5%** on your gross billed income.
*   **Income Limit:** You can remain in this regime as long as your annual billing does not exceed **$3.5 Million MXN** (Roughly 10-11 "Standard Tier" school sales per year).
*   **No Deductions Needed:** Because the tax rate is so low, the SAT does not allow you to deduct business expenses (like your internet, computer, or Vercel hosting) against your ISR. For software developers, our profit margins are nearly 100% of the sale price, so this is a massive advantage.

### How Much Will You Pay? (Example)
If you sell one "Standard Tier" package for **$300,000 MXN**:
*   **ISR (Income Tax):** You will pay between $3,000 and $7,500 MXN (1% to 2.5%) directly to the SAT.
*   **IVA (Value Added Tax):** You must still charge the school 16% IVA on top of your $300k fee. This $48,000 MXN is not your money; you collect it and pass it to the government. *However, you can deduct the IVA from your own business purchases (like your IONOS domain or new laptop).*

### Critical RESICO Requirements:
To qualify for RESICO, you **CANNOT**:
1.  Be a partner, shareholder, or board member of a registered corporation (Persona Moral).
2.  Have your fiscal residence outside of Mexico.

## The Alternative: Régimen de Actividades Empresariales y Profesionales
If you exceed the $3.5 Million MXN annual limit, or if you form a legal corporation with partners, you will default to this regime.

*   **ISR (Income Tax):** Progressive rate from **1.92% up to 35%**, calculated on your *profit* (Income minus Deductible Expenses).
*   **Why it's worse for solo devs:** To lower your tax bill from 35%, you have to spend a lot of money on deductible business expenses. Since building websites only costs time and minimal server fees, you will end up paying massive amounts of tax.

## Action Items for Operations
1.  **Update your Constancia de Situación Fiscal:** Log into the SAT portal and ensure your economic activity is registered under "Desarrollo de software", "Diseño de sistemas informáticos", or similar, and that your regime is **Régimen Simplificado de Confianza**.
2.  **The 1.25% Retention:** When you issue your PPD invoice (Factura) to a private school (which is a Persona Moral), the law requires the school to withhold **1.25% of your ISR** and pay it to the SAT on your behalf. Make sure your CFDI 4.0 invoice reflects this `Retención de ISR`.

## FAQ: Why not the "Digital Platforms" Regime?
You might have heard of the **Régimen de Plataformas Tecnológicas** (which has a flat 1% ISR retention). 

**Why we do not qualify for this:**
This regime is strictly for people who sell services or goods **through a digital intermediary** that connects buyers and sellers (e.g., selling freelance hours on Upwork/Fiverr, selling apps on the Apple App Store, or driving for Uber). In those cases, the platform collects the money and retains the taxes before paying you.

Because IDS Works operates as a direct B2B agency (you find the schools, you invoice them directly, and they deposit the money directly into your bank account), there is no digital intermediary. Therefore, you legally fall under **RESICO**, not Plataformas Tecnológicas. 

*Warning:* The SAT rules state that if you earn even $1 MXN through the Plataformas Tecnológicas regime (e.g., you do a side job on Upwork), you are **legally disqualified from using RESICO** for your direct agency clients, and you will be forced into the expensive *Actividad Empresarial* regime (up to 35% ISR). If you are running this agency, do not sell services on freelance marketplaces under the same RFC.
